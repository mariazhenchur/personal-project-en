Vocabulary

fingers keepers - said by somebody, usually a child, who has found an object of another person who has lost it, to show that they intent to keep it. to have a head start - to get an advantage that people have over other people in something such as a competition or race. to play hard to get - to pretend that you've less interested in somebody that you really are. to tip somebody off - to warn somebody secretly about something that will happen so that they can take action. to know better than to do something - to be wise or moral enough not to do something. to get the hump - to get upset and annoyed with somebody you think have done something bad for you. minted - recently produced or made. to pick up the pieces - to try to restore a situation to normality after a tragedy or trauma.
